Om Space internship | Propulsion testing

Small-Scale Rocket Engine Test Facility

Internship study on the facilities required to conduct small-scale rocket engine testing, including electric pump engine principles, test stand assembly, storage tanks, tubing, fittings, controls, and data acquisition.

Read case study View report
Period
After BTech studies
Focus
Test stand, feed system, controls, data acquisition
Project type
Additional project with future image space

Scope

The work studied what a small rocket engine test setup needs before firing can be conducted safely: propellant storage, pressurised feed paths, mechanical test stand layout, instrumentation, control logic, and data capture.

Approach

  • Reviewed electric pump engine principles and the role of each propulsion subsystem.
  • Mapped major facility elements, including storage tanks, tubing, fittings, and support structure.
  • Studied test stand assembly and how engine loads, access, and safety clearances affect layout.
  • Considered controls and data acquisition as part of repeatable test operations.

Engineering Value

Facility Thinking

Linked engine requirements to the ground support equipment needed to run a safe test.

Subsystem Awareness

Connected tanks, tubing, fittings, controls, and data acquisition into one facility-level view.

Safety Framing

Placed test stand assembly and operations inside a controlled, instrumentation-led environment.
