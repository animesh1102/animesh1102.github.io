Electric Taxiing System (Airbus Project)

Mechanical Design | Integration | Performance Analysis

Developed an electric taxiing system integrated into landing gear to reduce fuel consumption and emissions during ground operations.

Note: Detailed design confidential. High-level approach shown.

Problem

Design an onboard electric propulsion system capable of taxiing an aircraft independently of main engines.

Approach

  • Integrated electric motor into main landing gear
  • Designed torque transmission using gearbox system
  • Evaluated power requirements and efficiency
  • Assessed system weight and impact on aircraft performance

Engineering Work

Motor Selection

Torque and power sizing based on aircraft weight

Transmission

Gearbox design for torque transfer

Integration

Landing gear constraints and structural considerations

Performance

Fuel savings and operational efficiency analysis

CAD & Design Visuals

Engineering Insight

Electric taxiing improves fuel efficiency but introduces weight and integration challenges. The design requires balancing performance gains against system complexity.
