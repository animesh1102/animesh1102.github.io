Electric Taxiing System (A320 Class)

Airbus-Supported Project | Aircraft Systems Integration

Due to project confidentiality, detailed schematics and proprietary data are not included. This page focuses on engineering methodology and system-level design decisions.

1. Overview

This project involved the design and integration of an electric taxiing system to enable aircraft ground movement without main engine usage. The objective was to reduce fuel consumption, emissions, and operational costs during taxi operations.

2. System Concept

  • Electric motors integrated into main landing gear
  • Power supplied via aircraft APU
  • Independent wheel drive for manoeuvrability
  • Gear reduction system for torque amplification

3. Engineering Work

Motor Sizing

Calculated required torque (~12,500 Nm per wheel) based on rolling resistance, taxi acceleration, and incline conditions.

Motor Selection

Selected EMRAX 348 axial flux motor due to high power density and low mass, delivering ~400 kW peak output.

Gearbox Design

Designed two-stage planetary gearbox (~16:1 ratio) to convert high motor RPM into required wheel torque.

Integration

Integrated motor and gearbox within landing gear while maintaining structural and geometric constraints of the aircraft.

4. Performance

  • Total power requirement: ~237 kW
  • Motor mass: ~41 kg
  • System mass: ~90 kg per wheel
  • Fuel penalty: ~0.1% per mission

5. Engineering Considerations

  • Thermal management of motor and gearbox
  • Structural load transfer through landing gear
  • Power availability from APU
  • Redundancy and safety under failure conditions

6. Trade-Offs

  • Weight vs fuel savings
  • System complexity vs operational benefit
  • Integration constraints vs performance

8. Outcome

The system demonstrated feasibility of electric taxiing with manageable integration penalties and strong operational benefits, particularly in fuel savings and emissions reduction.
