Airbus Sponsored Project

Electric Taxiing System Integration (A320 Class)

Design and integration of an onboard electric taxiing system to eliminate engine-powered ground operations, reducing emissions, fuel burn, and operational costs.

Project Overview

This project focused on the development of an onboard electric taxiing (e-taxi) system for an A320-class aircraft. The objective was to enable autonomous ground movement using electric propulsion integrated into the landing gear, eliminating the need for main engine taxiing.

The work involved motor sizing, gearbox design, and system-level integration while considering aircraft constraints such as weight, power availability, and safety compliance.

Key Engineering Contributions

Motor Sizing

Determined torque requirement of ~25,000 Nm total (~12,500 Nm per wheel) based on rolling resistance, slope, and acceleration conditions.

Motor Selection

Selected EMRAX 348 AFPM motor for its high power density, achieving ~400 kW peak power with low mass (~41 kg).

Gearbox Design

Designed a two-stage planetary gearbox (~16:1 ratio) to convert high RPM (~4000) into required high torque output.

Landing Gear Integration

Integrated motor and gearbox within the main landing gear, ensuring compatibility with wheel hub geometry and retraction envelope.

Performance & Results

Power Requirement

Total system power requirement ~237 kW during taxiing, supplied via aircraft APU systems.

System Mass

Total added mass ~90 kg per wheel including motor and gearbox, within acceptable aircraft limits.

Fuel Impact

Estimated fuel penalty ~0.1% per mission, significantly offset by taxi fuel savings.

Safety

Gear system designed with load factors well above 1.5, ensuring structural safety under operational loads.
