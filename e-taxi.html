Electric Taxiing System

Landing-gear-integrated electric taxiing concept to reduce main-engine ground operation, with attention to motor sizing, gearbox transmission, packaging, and system-level aircraft impact.

Read case study Back to projects
Focus
Motor sizing, gearbox design, landing gear integration
Context
Airbus-supported Cranfield group project
Outcome
Concept trade study for fuel and emissions reduction

Problem

Design an onboard electric propulsion concept capable of moving the aircraft during taxi without relying on main engine thrust, while respecting the landing gear environment and aircraft-level weight penalties.

Detailed design data is confidential, so the page shows the public engineering structure rather than protected geometry or values.

Approach

  • Estimated the torque and power needed for ground taxiing at aircraft scale.
  • Selected an electric motor concept around low-speed torque demand.
  • Created a transmission path through a gearbox to transfer torque into the wheel system.
  • Reviewed landing gear packaging, structure, maintainability, and installation constraints.
  • Balanced operational fuel saving against added mass, complexity, and safety impact.

Engineering Work

Motor Selection

Torque and power sizing based on aircraft ground movement and low-speed wheel demand.

Transmission

Gearbox concept to convert motor output into useful wheel torque within limited packaging space.

Integration

Landing gear constraints, clearance, mass, inspection access, and structural attachment considerations.

Performance

Fuel burn, emissions, taxi operation, and aircraft-level efficiency trade-offs.

Visual Space

Insight

Electric taxiing can reduce ground fuel burn and emissions, but the engineering challenge is not only motor performance. The concept has to earn back its weight, integration effort, safety complexity, and maintenance burden at aircraft level.
