Aircraft Fuel System Architecture (Airbus Project)

Systems Engineering | CS-25 Compliance | Concept Design

Designed a conceptual fuel system for an A320-class aircraft, including venting, inerting, and water management systems aligned with certification requirements.

Note: Detailed design is confidential. High-level engineering approach presented.

Problem

Develop a safe, reliable fuel system ensuring proper venting, inerting, and water management while complying with CS-25 certification requirements.

Approach

  • Defined system architecture for fuel storage and transfer
  • Designed venting system to maintain tank pressure limits
  • Integrated inerting system (OBIGGS concept)
  • Developed water drainage and contamination management strategy
  • Aligned design with CS-25 safety requirements

Engineering Work

System Architecture

Fuel tank layout, piping network, and subsystem integration

Safety Design

Fire prevention, pressure regulation, and redundancy

Certification

CS-25 compliance for fuel safety and system integrity

Trade-offs

Weight vs safety vs system complexity

System Visualisation

Engineering Insight

Fuel system design is safety-critical, requiring redundancy, pressure control, and strict certification compliance. Integration complexity increases with safety requirements.
