Aircraft Fuel System Architecture (Airbus Project)

Systems Engineering | CS-25 Compliance | Safety-Critical Design

Developed a conceptual fuel system architecture for an A320-class aircraft, including venting, inerting, and water management systems aligned with certification requirements.

Tools: System Architecture Design | CS-25 | Safety Analysis

Note: Detailed design is confidential. High-level engineering approach presented.

Problem

Design a fuel system that ensures safe storage, transfer, and delivery of fuel while maintaining tank pressure, preventing ignition risk, and complying with CS-25 certification requirements.

Approach

  • Defined system architecture for fuel storage and transfer
  • Designed venting system to maintain pressure equilibrium
  • Integrated inerting system (OBIGGS) to reduce flammability risk
  • Developed water drainage and contamination control strategy
  • Aligned all subsystems with CS-25 safety requirements

Engineering Work

System Architecture

Fuel tank configuration, piping network, and subsystem integration

Venting & Pressure

Maintaining pressure limits to avoid structural or operational risks

Inerting System

Reduction of oxygen concentration using OBIGGS concept

Safety & Certification

Compliance with CS-25 requirements for fuel system safety

System Visualisation

Engineering Insight

Fuel system design is inherently safety-critical. The integration of venting, inerting, and drainage systems ensures safe operation under varying flight conditions. System redundancy and certification constraints significantly influence design complexity.

Key Design Drivers

  • Safety requirements dominate system architecture
  • Pressure control is critical to tank integrity
  • Weight vs redundancy trade-offs
  • Certification constraints drive design decisions

Engineering Assessment

The system satisfies conceptual safety and performance requirements under standard operating conditions. However, detailed validation would require failure analysis, redundancy verification, and compliance testing.

Limitations

  • No detailed failure mode analysis (FMEA)
  • No dynamic fuel sloshing effects considered
  • Simplified system modelling
  • No hardware validation

Next Steps

  • Perform detailed FMEA and safety analysis
  • Model transient fuel behaviour
  • Validate system through simulation or testing
  • Develop detailed component-level design
