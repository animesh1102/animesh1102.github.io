Solar Thermal Propulsion System

Design and analysis of a solar thermal propulsion concept for small satellites, where absorbed solar energy heats the working fluid before expansion through a nozzle.

Read case study View report

Additional project with future image space

Scope

The project explored a propulsion concept for small satellites by using solar radiation as the heat source. The system stores thermal energy in a cavity and transfers it to a working fluid before the nozzle converts that thermal energy into kinetic energy.

Approach

  • Defined the solar thermal propulsion operating principle for small satellite use.
  • Studied indirect solar absorption and heat storage in a thermal cavity.
  • Connected propellant heating to nozzle exit kinetic energy and thrust production.
  • Compared system-level promise against mass, pointing, thermal, and packaging constraints.
