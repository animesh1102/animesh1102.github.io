A320 Wing Structural Analysis

Comprehensive structural analysis of a tapered transport aircraft wing using beam theory and numerical methods.

1. Overview

This project investigates the structural response of a semi-span A320-class wing subjected to aerodynamic loading. The study models lift distribution, computes internal loads, and evaluates bending stress along the span.

2. Wing Geometry

  • Wingspan: 35.8 m
  • Semi-span: 17.9 m
  • Root chord: 6 m
  • Tip chord: 1.5 m
  • Taper ratio: 0.25

3. Methodology

An elliptical lift distribution was assumed. Shear force and bending moment were obtained using numerical integration. A simplified spar model was used to estimate bending stress using classical beam theory.

4. Governing Equations

  • Lift distribution: Elliptical
  • Shear force: V = ∫ L dy
  • Bending moment: M = ∫ V dy
  • Stress: σ = M·y / I
  • Second moment of area: I = bh³ / 12

5. Results

Max Moment

~2.2 MN·m

Max Stress

~88 MPa

Yield

300 MPa

FoS

~3.4

6. Structural Interpretation

The highest bending stress occurs at the wing root, where bending moment is maximum. Stress reduces toward the tip due to decreasing internal loads and chord length. This validates the use of tapered wings for structural efficiency.

7. Limitations

  • No torsional effects considered
  • No material nonlinearities
  • Simplified rectangular spar model
  • No aeroelastic coupling

8. Conclusion

The wing design demonstrates safe structural performance with a factor of safety above 3. The analysis highlights the importance of load distribution and geometry in aircraft wing design.
